PART 1: THE TWO MODELS — WHICH ONE TO USE?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

MODEL A — MANAGED SERVICE (Recommended for beginners)
  → You sign up for Vapi. You own the account.
  → You build the bot in YOUR account.
  → You give the client a phone number or web widget.
  → You charge: Setup Fee + Monthly Flat + Per-Minute Markup.
  → Client NEVER sees your prompts or your Vapi login.
  → Your profit = the "spread" between what Vapi charges you vs what you bill.

MODEL B — CLIENT-OWNED ACCOUNT (Lower cash-flow risk)
  → You help the client create THEIR OWN Vapi account.
  → Their credit card is linked to Vapi directly.
  → You build the bot inside their account.
  → You charge a "Management Fee" of $200–$400/month.
  → Con: The client can see your prompts. Protect yourself with a contract.

PART 2: HIPAA — WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW BEFORE YOU SELL TO A CLINIC
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

What is HIPAA?
  HIPAA = Health Insurance Portability & Accountability Act (US law).
  Similar laws exist in the EU (GDPR) and UK (Data Protection Act).

The Rule:
  If your AI hears a patient say their name, phone number, date of birth,
  or medical complaint — that data MUST be encrypted and handled by a
  HIPAA-compliant provider.

The Risk:
  Selling a non-compliant bot to a US dental clinic and having a data leak
  can result in fines of $100–$50,000 PER RECORD. The dentist and potentially
  YOU could be liable.

The Solution:
  1. Use Vapi's HIPAA-compliant plan (~$1,000/year).
  2. Use Twilio for phone numbers (they sign a HIPAA Business Associate
     Agreement — BAA — for free).
  3. Build the cost of HIPAA compliance into your setup fee.
  4. Include a BAA clause in your client contract.

Non-US clients (India, UK, EU)?
  Fewer restrictions, but always encrypt data in transit and at rest.
  Use this as a selling point ("enterprise-grade security").

PART 3: STEP-BY-STEP — FROM ZERO TO FIRST PAYING CLIENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STEP 1 — Pick Your Niche (Day 1–2)
  Best niches for AI receptionists:
    • Dental clinics
    • Medical GP / GP offices
    • Real estate agencies
    • Law firms
    • Salons & spas
    • Physiotherapy / wellness studios

  Why these? They all have high call volume, appointments to book,
  and staff who hate answering repetitive calls.

  ACTION: Pick ONE niche. Do not try to sell to everyone at first.
  Example: "I only work with dental clinics in [Your City]."



STEP 2 — Build the Demo (Day 3–5)
  Sign up for Vapi at https://vapi.ai (free $10 credits to start).
  Create a simple "Dental Receptionist" bot:
    - Name: "Hi, you've reached [Clinic Name]. I'm your AI assistant!"
    - It should: state clinic hours, book appointments (or collect name+number),
      answer basic FAQs (insurance, parking, procedures).

  Test it: call the Twilio number yourself. Record it.
  Make it sound friendly, warm, and professional.

  TOOLS NEEDED:
    • Vapi account (vapi.ai)
    • Twilio number (~$1/month + calls)
    • Optional: Calendly or Cal.com for booking integration



STEP 3 — Find the Prospect (Day 5–7)
  Method 1 — Google Maps Scraping:
    Search "dental clinic [city]" on Google Maps.
    Note clinics with: no chatbot, slow website, generic phone greeting.
    These are your best targets.

  Method 2 — LinkedIn:
    Search for "practice manager" or "clinic owner" in your target city.
    Connect + send a short DM (see outreach script below).

  Method 3 — Cold Email:
    Find the clinic email on their website.
    Use the email template below.

  Method 4 — Walk In:
    Go to the clinic reception. Ask for the owner/manager.
    Say: "I build AI phone systems for dental clinics. Can I show you
    a 3-minute demo that could book more appointments automatically?"

STEP 5 — The Demo Meeting (Day 10–14)
  Show them:
    1. The PROBLEM: "How many calls do you miss after 5 PM?"
    2. Play the live bot demo (call the Twilio number).
    3. The COST comparison:
       - A human receptionist: $2,500–$4,000/month.
       - Your AI: $349/month + small setup fee.
    4. Show the Pricing page on your website.

  Close with:
    "I can have this live for your clinic in 5 working days.
     The setup is $1,000 — which covers configuration, your HIPAA
     compliance setup, and the dedicated phone number.
     The monthly plan is $349. Want to get started?"



STEP 6 — The Contract & Setup Fee
  ALWAYS get a signed contract before doing any work.
  Key clauses to include:
    1. Services rendered (AI bot, phone number, HIPAA setup).
    2. Setup fee: $1,000 (non-refundable after work begins).
    3. Monthly subscription: $349/month (billed via Stripe).
    4. Per-minute overage: $0.55/min above 200 mins/month.
    5. Termination: 30-day written notice.
    6. Data handling: You comply with HIPAA. BAA attached as Exhibit A.
    7. Intellectual Property: You retain all prompt/system IP.
       Client receives the output (phone number + bot access).

  Free contract template: https://www.docracy.com (search "SaaS contract")
  Or use a lawyer for $200–$500 to draft a reusable template.



STEP 7 — Delivery (The "Black Box" Method)
  • Create the bot in YOUR Vapi account (not the client's).
  • Connect a Twilio phone number to the bot.
  • Give the client ONLY:
      (a) The phone number to forward their main line to, OR
      (b) A web widget embed code for their website.
  • Client NEVER gets your Vapi login, your prompts, or your system config.
  • This protects your IP and keeps them dependent on you for updates.



STEP 8 — Monthly Billing (How You Always Get Paid)
  Set up Stripe (stripe.com) with a monthly subscription:
    1. Go to Stripe → Products → Create a subscription product.
    2. Add: $349/month flat fee (platform).
    3. Add: metered billing for per-minute overage.
    4. Client adds their card to Stripe checkout.
    5. At the end of each month:
       - Check your Vapi dashboard for usage.
       - Vapi charged you: X minutes × $0.20/min.
       - You charge client: X minutes × $0.55/min.
       - Upload the overage invoice to Stripe.

PART 5: COMMON OBJECTIONS & HOW TO HANDLE THEM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Q: "What if patients complain about talking to a robot?"
A: "The AI sounds natural and introduces itself transparently.
    Most callers appreciate instant answers over being put on hold.
    We also have a 'transfer to human' trigger if the patient prefers."

Q: "Is this HIPAA compliant?"
A: "Yes — we use Vapi's HIPAA plan and Twilio, both of which allow us
    to sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA). We include the BAA
    in our contract at no extra cost."

Q: "$1,000 setup is too much."
A: "I understand. Consider that a part-time receptionist answering after-hours
    calls costs $800–$1,500/month. The $1,000 setup is a one-time fee, and
    the monthly plan at $349 saves you that every single month."

Q: "What if the AI gets something wrong?"
A: "We start with a 2-week calibration period where we tune the script
    based on call recordings. You also get a monthly performance report.
    The bot only handles straightforward calls — anything complex is
    escalated to your human staff."

Q: "Can I cancel?"
A: "Yes — 30-day written notice, no penalty. But in our experience,
    once you see the calls it handles, you won't want to."
